# Visual Style Guide 2.0

Vision Statement
Mytherra should feel like stepping into a beautifully illustrated American folklore storybook brought to life.
Players should experience warmth before danger.
Hope before despair.
Wonder before action.
Every frame should look like a painting that invites exploration.
Art Direction
Style
Painterly Stylized Fantasy
NOT:
Photorealistic
Anime
Cartoon
Comic book
Instead:
Soft painterly brushwork
Rich natural lighting
Slightly exaggerated proportions
Detailed but readable environments
Handcrafted appearance
Reference feeling:
“A living illustrated novel.”

Layer 1
Natural Landscape
Mountains
Trees
Streams
Sky

Human History
Roads
Bridges
Cabins

Ruins
Layer 3
Spirit World
Shrines
Lantern light
Ancient carvings
Subtle magical energy
Layer 4
Living Details
Smoke from chimneys
Birds
Leaves blowing
Fireflies
Running water
Moving grass
Nothing should ever feel static.
Color Philosophy
Never use maximum saturation.
Natural colors.
Warm highlights.
Cool shadows.
Lanterns become the brightest warm light in almost every night scene.

Guardians

Material Language
Wood should look aged.
Stone should feel ancient.
Iron should show use.
Leather should feel handcrafted.
Magic should resemble light instead of explosions.

Occupation
Personality
Region
Life experience

Heavy wool
Leather

Loose fabrics
River colors

Weapons
Weapons should feel handcrafted.
Not oversized.
Not magical by default.
Examples:
Walking staffs
Forged axes
Old hunting bows
Traveler’s swords
Spirit magic comes from the Lantern—not from oversized glowing weapons.
Lantern Design
The Lantern is the visual icon of Mytherra.
Every player carries one.
It evolves over time.
Changes include:
Glass color
Metalwork
Spirit engravings
Light intensity
Resonance effects
A player should instantly recognize another Lantern Keeper by their lantern.

Animation
Movement should feel calm.
Characters have weight.
Lanterns sway.
Trees breathe.
Clothing reacts to wind.
Magic grows naturally instead of flashing instantly.
Signature Screenshot Test
Every screenshot should contain at least three of these:
Warm lantern light
Beautiful landscape
Visible mystery
Interesting silhouette
Living environment
Storytelling through scenery
If not, reconsider the composition.
Production Asset Standards
Environment Concepts 3840×2160
Character Concepts 2048×2048
Portraits 1024×1024
Battle Sprites 512×512 master (scaled down for gameplay)
Player Sprites 48×48 master, exported to game resolution
Icons 256×256 master
Tiles 48×48 master, exported as needed
Always keep a high-resolution master before creating game-optimized versions.
The Three-Layer Asset Pipeline
Every visual asset follows the same progression:
Layer 1 – Concept Defines mood, proportions, and storytelling.
Layer 2 – Production Master Clean, isolated, transparent, high-resolution source.
Layer 3 – Game Asset Optimized sprite, icon, tile, portrait, or UI element referenced by the engine.
Never use concept art directly in-game.
Definition of Success
A player should be able to recognize Mytherra from a single screenshot because of its warm lantern glow, painterly landscapes, grounded folklore, and sense of quiet wonder.
