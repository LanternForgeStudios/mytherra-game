# Combat Bible v1.0

Combat Philosophy
Combat should feel:
Strategic rather than frantic
Easy to learn
Difficult to master
Rewarding for preparation
Connected to the game’s story
The player should rarely win by simply selecting “Attack” every turn.
Knowledge, preparation, teamwork, and understanding the enemy should matter.
Core Combat Loop
Encounter begins
Enemy is identified
Journal displays known lore (if previously encountered)
Players choose actions
Turn order is determined
Actions resolve
Status effects update
Victory, defeat, or alternate resolution
Rewards and Journal updates
Battle Types
Standard Encounter
Random Echoes and wildlife.
Duration: 2–5 minutes.
Purpose: XP, exploration, materials.
Elite Encounter
Rare enemies.
Unique mechanics.
Higher rewards.
Often tied to side quests.
Guardian Encounter
Major story battles.
Multiple combat phases.
Dialogue during battle.
Environmental mechanics.
May end through combat or restoration.
Expedition Encounter
Multiplayer co-op.
Up to four Lantern Keepers.
Unique rewards.
Shared objectives.
World Events
Server-wide encounters.
Many parties contribute toward a shared goal.
Progress affects the world for all players.

Boss Design Principles
Every boss introduces a new mechanic.
Examples:
Changing weather
Shrine activation
Summoned allies
Terrain hazards
Dialogue choices
Memory phases
Puzzle mechanics
Bosses should feel like stories rather than stat checks.
Multiplayer Combat
Party Size: 1–4
Roles are flexible rather than rigid.
Every Lantern Keeper should contribute through:
Damage
Support
Spirit Arts
Observation
Restoration
No traditional “tank/healer” requirement.
Encourage creative team composition.
Victory Conditions
Not every battle ends by defeating the opponent.
Possible victory types include:
Defeat all enemies
Restore the Echo
Protect an NPC
Survive until dawn
Seal a corrupted shrine
Complete a ritual
Escape safely
The game should regularly surprise players with alternative objectives.
Defeat
Defeat should create setbacks, not frustration.
Possible consequences:
Return to the nearest Lantern Hall
Lose some carried gold
Temporary equipment damage
Story consequences in specific quests
Never lose permanent progression.

Combat Design Rules
Every battle should teach something.
Every boss should tell a story.
Preparation should matter more than grinding.
Observation should be rewarded.
Knowledge should become a form of power.
The best Lantern Keepers are remembered not because they defeated every legend—but because they understood them.
