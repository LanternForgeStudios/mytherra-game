# Player Experience Bible v1.0

Purpose
The Player Experience Bible defines how Mytherra should feel to play.
Every system, quest, piece of music, visual asset, and gameplay mechanic should reinforce these experiences.
If a feature does not improve the player experience described here, it should be reconsidered.
The Core Fantasy
The player is not a conqueror.
The player is a Lantern Keeper.
A Lantern Keeper explores forgotten places, restores harmony, uncovers hidden truths, and preserves the stories of Mytherra.
Strength comes from understanding the world—not simply overpowering it.
The Five Emotional Pillars
Wonder
Players should regularly discover places, creatures, stories, or mechanics they did not expect.
Questions are more valuable than immediate answers.
Examples:
A mysterious shrine hidden behind a waterfall.
A spirit that reacts differently depending on the season.
An abandoned cabin with journal pages that hint at a forgotten legend.
Discovery
Every play session should reward curiosity.
Players should be encouraged to leave the main path.
Examples:
Hidden shortcuts.
Secret Journal entries.
Optional NPC conversations.
Ancient inscriptions.
Rare gathering nodes.
Discovery should never feel like wasted time.
Restoration
The player’s actions improve the world.
Examples:
A restored shrine brings wildlife back.
A repaired bridge reconnects two villages.
Calming an Echo makes nearby travel safer.
Completing a regional story changes the environment.
Players should feel they leave Mytherra better than they found it.
Community
Multiplayer exists to create shared stories.
Players should naturally:
help each other
celebrate discoveries
form Lodges
explore together
participate in world events
Competition is optional.
Cooperation is encouraged.
Mystery
Not everything should be explained.
Some mysteries should take months—or even years—to fully understand.
Examples:
The true names of the First Ones.
The meaning of Elyric inscriptions.
The cause of the Great Silence.
The origins of Resonance.
The community should enjoy theorizing together.
Player Session Goals
Every 5 Minutes
The player should experience at least one meaningful interaction.
Examples:
A battle.
A new NPC.
A Journal update.
A hidden path.
A puzzle.
A memorable line of dialogue.
Never allow long stretches of repetitive travel.
Every 15 Minutes
The player should make visible progress.
Examples:
Complete a quest.
Gain a level.
Unlock a Spirit Art.
Upgrade equipment.
Restore a shrine.
Discover a new landmark.
Deepen a relationship with an NPC.
Progress should feel tangible.
Every Hour
The player should experience something memorable.
Examples:
A Guardian encounter.
A major story reveal.
A breathtaking landscape.
A multiplayer world event.
A difficult moral choice.
A rare Resonance interaction.
These are the moments players tell their friends about.
Exploration Philosophy
Exploration should always answer one of four questions:
“What happened here?”
“What is this place?”
“Who lived here?”
“What can I learn?”
Exploration should rarely exist only to collect loot.
Combat Philosophy
Combat should create stories.
Every encounter should teach the player something.
Players should regularly choose between:
fighting
observing
soothing
protecting
escaping
Not every victory requires defeating an enemy.
NPC Philosophy
NPCs are people, not quest markers.
Every important NPC should have:
a routine
relationships
fears
hopes
memories
opinions about the player
Returning to towns should feel like visiting familiar people.
Progression Philosophy
Power comes from multiple sources.
Character progression includes:
Levels
Equipment
Spirit Arts
Resonance
Harmony
Journal completion
Regional reputation
No single progression system should dominate.
Multiplayer Philosophy
Players should feel like fellow explorers.
Multiplayer should answer: “How can another player make this journey more meaningful?”
Examples:
Shared expeditions.
Cooperative puzzles.
World restoration events.
Lodge projects.
Trading discoveries.
Helping newcomers.
Avoid mechanics that encourage toxic competition.
Visual Experience
Every region should communicate a distinct emotional identity.
Ash Hollow: Warm, welcoming, safe.
Iron Forest: Curious, quiet, mysterious.
Old Creek Shrine: Ancient, hopeful, sacred.
The environment should tell stories before NPCs speak.
Audio Experience
Silence is a tool.
Ambient sounds should often carry exploration.
Music should support emotion rather than overwhelm it.
The Lantern should have recognizable audio cues that become comforting over time.
Journal of Legends
The Journal is not a checklist.
It is the player’s personal chronicle.
Every meaningful discovery should feel like adding another page to a living story.
The Player Compass
At the end of every play session, the player should be able to answer “yes” to most of these questions:
Did I discover something new?
Did I improve my Lantern Keeper?
Did I learn something about Mytherra?
Did I make the world better?
Did I encounter something beautiful, mysterious, or memorable?
Do I have a reason to come back tomorrow?
If the answer is “no” too often, the game has drifted away from its vision.
Definition of Success
A successful Lantern Keeper is not remembered for defeating the most enemies.
They are remembered because they uncovered forgotten truths, restored balance, built friendships, and left Mytherra brighter than they found it.
That is the experience every player should feel from their very first lantern to their final journey.
